Local Expertise

Neighborhoods I Know Inside & Out

Old Village

Old Village is the heart of Mount Pleasant's historic character. Tree-lined streets anchored by century-old live oaks, charming cottages alongside gracious estates, and a distinct sense of permanence define this neighborhood. The Historic District has evolved thoughtfully—preserving architectural integrity while allowing for sensitive renovations and new construction that respects neighborhood vernacular.

What makes Old Village special is its walkability and authenticity. The village center hosts local shops, restaurants, and gathering spaces that foster genuine community. You'll encounter neighbors on morning walks, find yourself at the same coffee shop regularly, and feel the kind of connectedness that many residential neighborhoods aspire to but rarely achieve. Properties here range from modest historic homes to significant estates, but all sit within a framework of respectful stewardship.

Old Mount Pleasant

Old Mount Pleasant stretches beyond the Village proper, encompassing established neighborhoods with deep roots and a distinctly Lowcountry feel. These tree-canopied streets feature homes built across several decades—many from the mid-20th century—situated on larger lots than newer developments allow. The neighborhood has maintained its residential character while quietly appreciating in value, drawing families and empty-nesters who value proximity to the water, established landscaping, and the sense of living in a real community.

This area appeals to buyers seeking a step back from the intensity of newer developments without sacrificing convenience. You're close to shopping, dining, and waterfront access, but removed from the bustle. Many properties benefit from mature landscaping, updated interiors, and lot sizes that allow for meaningful outdoor living. The neighborhood is experiencing selective renovation without losing its character—a delicate balance that makes it increasingly appealing to discerning buyers.

I'On

I'On represents a different kind of character—a master-planned, New Urbanism-inspired community that has become one of the Lowcountry's most distinctive neighborhoods. Developed with thoughtful attention to architecture, public spaces, and walkability, I'On features homes built in a cohesive architectural language inspired by historic Lowcountry design, but with modern systems and conveniences. Tree-lined streets, pocket parks, a town center, and carefully maintained common areas create an intentional sense of place.

What appeals to I'On buyers is the combination of new construction quality with established neighborhood appeal—homes that feel like they've always belonged here. The community attracts families, young professionals, and empty-nesters who value walkability, architectural consistency, and a curated aesthetic. I'On is larger and more suburban than Old Village, but with far more design intention than typical developments. If you're seeking new construction with character, modern amenities with historic soul, and a community that feels both energetic and settled, I'On delivers.
